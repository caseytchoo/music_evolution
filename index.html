Abstract

This study examines how in what ways popular music has evolved from 1950 to 2019. Using a large dataset containing attributes such as loudness, valence, energy, and genre, we analyzed long-term trends in sound and mood. Results show that average loudness has steadily increased, while emotional positivity (valence) has slightly declined over time. Genre diversity has also expanded, reflecting broader cultural and technological influences. These findings highlight how changes in production, technology, and audience preferences have shaped the evolution of modern music.
